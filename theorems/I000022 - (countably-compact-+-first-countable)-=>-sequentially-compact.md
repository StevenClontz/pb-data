If $A=\{x_n\}$ is a sequence in $X$ then since $X$ is countably compact, $A$ has a limit point $x$. If $\{B_i\}$ is a countable local basis at $x$, then taking a $x_{n_i} \in B_i$ gives a subsequence of $\{x_n\}$ converging to $x$.
